## Course frame / Marco del curso / Cadrul cursului

**EN:** Ancient Greek and Roman architecture should be studied as a system of materials, proportions, ritual, and civic display, not just as a sequence of monuments. Vitruvius is the indispensable primary source because his ten books cover materials, orders, temples, public buildings, houses, decoration, and engineering. [archive](https://archive.org/details/vitruvius-on-architecture)

## 1. Introducere / Introducción / Introduction

### Core readings
- **Vitruvius, *On Architecture / De Architectura*** — the best starting point for terminology, orders, building types, and theory; Harvard’s edition notes the book structure clearly, including temples, materials, public buildings, houses, decoration, and engineering. [hup.harvard](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/books/9780674992771)
- **Mark Wilson Jones, *Origins of Classical Architecture*** — excellent for the origins and logic of the Greek orders, especially the relationship between temples, gifts, and the emergence of Doric, Ionic, and Corinthian form. [books.google](https://books.google.com/books/about/Origins_of_Classical_Architecture.html?id=RWuVnAEACAAJ)
- **Robin Osborne, *Archaic and Classical Greek Art*** — useful for framing architecture alongside sculpture and visual culture in Greece from the Geometric period to the early Hellenistic world. [barnesandnoble](https://www.barnesandnoble.com/w/archaic-and-classical-greek-art-robin-osborne/1100567639?ean=9780192842022)

### Key concepts
- Architectural profession in antiquity, especially the move from practice to theory through Vitruvius. [archive](https://archive.org/details/vitruvius-on-architecture)
- Materials and tectonics: stone, marble, concrete, and masonry systems, with Roman innovation especially visible in arches, vaults, and domes. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNjGJ9aIZ7g)
- Proportion, symmetry, optical correction, color, and sculptural ornament as constitutive elements of design. [archive](https://archive.org/details/vitruvius-on-architecture)
- Orders as historical systems rather than static “styles”. [intbau](https://www.intbau.org/members/mark-wilson-jones/)

## 2. Sacred architecture / Arquitectura sagrada / Arhitectura de cult

### Core readings
- **Mark Wilson Jones, *Origins of Classical Architecture*** — especially strong for Greek temples and the ritual logic behind the orders. [books.google](https://books.google.com/books/about/Origins_of_Classical_Architecture.html?id=RWuVnAEACAAJ)
- **Vitruvius, books 3–4** in *On Architecture* — important for temple typologies and the classical orders as theory. [hup.harvard](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/books/9780674992771)
- **Clemente Marconi** — useful for advanced scholarship on ancient architecture; his profile at NYU/Academia.edu anchors a research-oriented pathway into ancient architectural studies. [nyu.academia](https://nyu.academia.edu/ClementeMarconi/The%20Journal%20of%20Ancient%20Architecture)
- **Mantha Zarmakoupi** — valuable for Roman architecture and urbanism, especially the broader Mediterranean context of sacred and civic space. [ias](https://www.ias.edu/scholars/mantha-zarmakoupi)

### Suggested sequence
- Greek temple: structure, function, sacred setting.  
- Archaic, Classical, and Hellenistic evolution of the temple.  
- Roman temple: continuity plus innovation in plan, elevation, and setting.  
- Early Christian spaces and subterranean architectures: catacombs and mithraea.  

### Useful lens
Study temples as **ritual machines**: they are not just façades with columns, but spatial devices that mediate procession, visibility, offering, and sacred presence.

## 3. Civic architecture / Arquitectura cívica / Arhitectura civilă

### Core readings
- **Vitruvius, book 5** for fora, basilicae, theaters, colonnades, baths, and harbors. [hup.harvard](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/books/9780674992771)
- **Vitruvius, book 6** for domestic architecture and the Roman house. [hup.harvard](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/books/9780674992771)
- **Robin Osborne** for the Greek city and the social context of architectural production. [en.wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robin_Osborne)
- **Mantha Zarmakoupi** for Roman urbanism, especially Delos and the infrastructure of ancient Mediterranean cities. [manthazarmakoupi](https://manthazarmakoupi.com/cv/)

### Topics
- Acropolis, sanctuary, agora, stoa, forum.  
- Theater, amphitheater, baths, and spectacle.  
- Mausolea, altars, triumphal arches, columns, commemorative architecture.  
- Domus, villa, and insula as models of habitation.

### How to use them
- For **Greek theory and orders**, prioritize Wilson Jones and Osborne. [books.google](https://books.google.com/books/about/Origins_of_Classical_Architecture.html?id=RWuVnAEACAAJ)

- For **research-level writing**, look at Marconi’s academic profile and related work. [nyu.academia](https://nyu.academia.edu/ClementeMarconi/The%20Journal%20of%20Ancient%20Architecture)

## 6. Study path / Ruta de estudio / Rută de studiu

### Module 1
Read Vitruvius alongside a glossary of terms: column, capital, entablature, cella, pronaos, peristyle, arch, vault, dome, opus caementicium. [hup.harvard](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/books/9780674992771)

### Module 2
Move to Greek architecture through the temple and the orders, using Wilson Jones as the main guide and Osborne for the broader artistic context. [barnesandnoble](https://www.barnesandnoble.com/w/archaic-and-classical-greek-art-robin-osborne/1100567639?ean=9780192842022)

### Module 3
Shift to Rome: public building, urbanism, domestic architecture, and technical innovation, with Vitruvius plus Zarmakoupi. [ias](https://www.ias.edu/scholars/mantha-zarmakoupi)

### Module 4
Watch lecture content from YaleCourses and Ancient Architects while reading selected chapters and articles; this is where monument analysis becomes much easier. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/ancientarchitects)
